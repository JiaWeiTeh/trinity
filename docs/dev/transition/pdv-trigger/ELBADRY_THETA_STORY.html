Chapter 1

The master parameter θ, and El-Badry's closed form

TRINITY transitions a feedback bubble from energy-driven (a hot, thermally-supported interior does PdV work on the shell) to momentum-driven (the hot gas has radiated away; only the wind's direct ram pressure remains) when the bubble becomes cooling-dominated. The single number that measures "cooling-dominated" is

$$\theta \;\equiv\; \frac{L_{\rm cool}}{L_{\rm mech}}\qquad(\text{fraction of the mechanical luminosity radiated away}).$$

θ is identical across the three sources we lean on: TRINITY's cooling_balance trigger, El-Badry+2019 ($\theta=L_{\rm int}/\dot E_{\rm in}$), and Lancaster ($\theta=\dot E_{\rm cool}/L_w$). All the bubble dynamics follow through the factor $(1-\theta)$ — the fraction of energy left to drive the shell.

The problem this workstream exists to solve: TRINITY's native radiative cooling under-cools. Its resolved θ peaks near 0.66 for compact/dense clouds (FINDINGS §6a) — well below the 0.95 trigger — so realistic clouds never transition, even though El-Badry and Lancaster both say θ should be ~0.9–0.99 for them. So we impose a physically-motivated θ. El-Badry+2019 gives a closed form for it (their Eq 37/38):

$$\boxed{\;\theta(n) \;=\; \frac{A_{\rm mix}\,\sqrt{\lambda\delta v\,n}}{\tfrac{11}{5} + A_{\rm mix}\,\sqrt{\lambda\delta v\,n}}\;}$$

symbolmeaningvalue we set
$A_{\rm mix}$mixing-layer normalization (fit; 1.7 analytic)3.5 (El-Badry Eq 37 fit)
$\lambda\delta v$mixing length × velocity (pc·km/s) — the one free knob3.0 (calibrated to Lancaster GMC θ≈0.9–0.99)
$n$local gas density at the shell, get_density_profile(R2) → cm⁻³per-step, per-config
$\theta_{\max}$saturation cap0.99

So "El-Badry θ=0.99 imposed" is shorthand for: for a dense cloud, the √n term saturates the closed form, θ pins at the cap 0.99, and the model is told to radiate 99% of Lmech — leaving 1% to drive the shell.

Chapter 2

What we IMPOSE — effective_Lloss

The shadow harness (data/_theta_elbadry_runner.py) monkeypatches one function, effective_Lloss_from_params (bound in both get_betadelta.py and run_energy_implicit_phase.py), so every solver step uses:

theta   = A_mix * sqrt(lambda_dv * n_amb(R2)) / (11/5 + A_mix * sqrt(...))   # capped at 0.99
L_loss  = max( L_cool + L_leak,   theta * L_mech )     # <- what we impose

This reuses TRINITY's existing theta_target mode exactly (get_betadelta.py:355): the max() guarantees we never reduce cooling below what TRINITY resolves — we only lift it up to the El-Badry target. The imposed Lloss then feeds consistently into the (β,δ) residual, the Edot_from_balance energy ODE, and the trigger.

Chapter 3

What we CHECK — the cooling_balance trigger

The transition fires (run_energy_implicit_phase.py:1207, and now also in phase 1a) when the net energy fraction drops below a threshold:

$$\frac{L_{\rm gain}-L_{\rm loss}}{L_{\rm gain}} < 0.05 \qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad \theta \ge 0.95$$

with $L_{\rm gain}=L_{\rm mech}$. So the "0.95 trigger" and "net energy < 5%" are the same statement. Imposing the El-Badry θ is exactly what pushes a cloud across it.

Chapter 4

The physics behind the √(λδv·n) form

El-Badry+2019 models the bubble's cooling as happening in a turbulent mixing layer at the interface between the hot ($10^6$–$10^7$ K) interior and the cool ($\sim10^4$ K) shell. Hot and cool gas mix at an intermediate temperature $T_{\rm pk}\approx2\times10^4$ K where the cooling function peaks; the layer radiates efficiently there. The mixing rate scales as the product of a mixing length and a velocity ($\lambda\delta v$), and the emission measure of the layer scales with density $n$ — so the radiated luminosity, and hence θ, grows as $\sqrt{\lambda\delta v\,n}$. The $11/5$ and $A_{\rm mix}$ come from integrating the cooling function through the layer (El-Badry §3.1/§5.2, Eq 16/19/20/21/35/37/38; verified from the maintainer-supplied PDF — ELBADRY_REFERENCE.md).

Regime caveat (this is the crux of Chapter 6). El-Badry's θ is a radiative ratio, derived for SN-driven bubbles where the interior is hot and tenuous and radiative mixing-layer cooling is the dominant sink. It says nothing about PdV / inertial losses. When TRINITY's dominant sink is PdV work on a heavy shell (massive clouds), the El-Badry radiative θ is off-regime.

Two independent anchors agree the magnitude is right where it applies: Lancaster's 3D GMC sims give θ≈0.9–0.99 for nH≈40–2×10⁵, and calibrating λδv≈3 lands El-Badry's θ in that band (within ~15%). This is why λδv=3 is the chosen knob (LANCASTER_REFERENCE.md §7).

The reversal — imposing θ fights the PR#715 handoff

The maintainer merged bugfix/high-mass-cluster-transition-without-ebpeak (PR #715) to main: phase 1b now routes a finite $E_b\le0$ collapse to the momentum phase (classify_energy_collapse, ENERGY_HANDOFF_FLOOR=1e3) instead of dead-stopping as ENERGY_COLLAPSED, and phase 1a gains a cooling_balance parity check. I merged main in and re-ran.

Why? Because the high-mass turnover is PdV/inertia-driven, not radiative — and imposing a radiative θ there double-counts the loss:

Consequence. The theta_elbadry SPEC, as written (impose θ on every cloud), re-breaks exactly the massive clouds PR #715 just fixed. El-Badry's θ is physical only where radiative cooling dominates (dense/compact). The mode needs a regime gate — off when PdV/Lmech≳1, deferring those clouds to the momentum handoff. Stage B (production wiring) is not ready for the massive-cloud regime. (FINDINGS §8b.)
Chapter 7

Open question — does v2<0 mean momentum?

open When the shell reverses ($v_2<0$), should the run jump straight to the momentum phase (dropping the cooling/structure solve)? It would prevent the solver hang seen at the collapse singularity. But thinking critically: no, $v_2<0$ does not by itself mean momentum-driven.

  • The energy↔momentum distinction is about the thermal reservoir $E_b$, not the velocity sign. "Momentum-driven" means $E_b\to0$ so the bubble pressure floors at the wind ram pressure. A bubble with $E_b>0$ and $v_2<0$ is a thermally-supported shell that is recollapsing because gravity + swept-up inertia + ambient pressure currently exceed $4\pi R_2^2 P_b$ — it is still energy-driven, and physical.
  • Such a bubble can even rebound: as it contracts it does negative PdV work on the interior, $E_b$ (and $P_b$) rise, resisting collapse — an oscillatory "breathing" mode. Forcing it to momentum on the first $v_2<0$ would destroy that physics and mislabel the state.
  • The correct transition criterion is therefore the maintainer's $E_b$-based one (thermal energy spent → momentum). A collapsing $E_b>0$ bubble that keeps shrinking is a genuine SHELL_COLLAPSED (the cloud recollapses to re-form stars), not a momentum handoff.
So the solver hang is an $E_b$-singularity issue, not a velocity one. The structure solve goes singular only as $E_b\to0$ ($R_1\to R_2$, shell_volume→0). In the θ-imposed runs the over-cooling drove $E_b$ negative, and the transition phase kept solving the singular structure (that is the hang). The robust fix is to check $E_b$ / the pressure-crossover before the expensive structure solve each step and route then — the "pressure-crossover terminal event" the maintainer already flagged as deferred (HIMASS_HANDOFF_PLAN.md §2) — not a $v_2<0$ trigger. My earlier "$v_2<0$ → momentum" suggestion is retracted.
