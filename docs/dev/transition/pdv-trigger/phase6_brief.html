f_A Phase-6 brief — what the knobs are, what was measured, what's next

2026-07-19 · TRINITY pdv-trigger workstream · sources: FINDINGS.md §15e–§15j, SOURCE_TERM_DESIGN.md §1–§4, data/bench6_analysis.csv. Verified against source at bubble_luminosity.py:435/845, get_betadelta.py:334–357.

1. The stage: TRINITY's cooling budget in one picture

Inside the energy-driven phase, TRINITY solves the Weaver-style bubble structure T(r) every step and splits the radiative loss by temperature zone:

Lcool = L₁ + L₂ + L₃
  • L₁ — the hot interior (T > 105.5 K): shocked-wind gas in CIE. Radiates weakly; its emission scales with the bubble volume.
  • L₂ + L₃ — the conduction front (10⁴ < T < 105.5 K): the thin bubble-side layer draped on the contact discontinuity at R₂. Precision (2026-07-19): the interface surface itself is the CD — L₃'s outer edge against the shell's inner skin (shell_structure); dMdt is mass ablated FROM the shell, transiting L₃→L₂ into the hot bubble (bubble_dMdt, "mass flux from shell into hot region"). L₂+L₃ is the radiative emission OF that front — dense, non-CIE, on the cooling-curve peak — and it scales with the CD area. The shell-side skin radiates the stellar budget, not Lmech, so it never enters θ.

Two more players complete the budget: Lleak (bulk hot-gas escape through shell holes — Rogers & Pittard channel; not radiation) and Lmech (the SB99 wind+SN input). The transition trigger watches the instantaneous cooling fraction

θ = Lloss / Lmech ,   fire when θ ≥ 0.95  (cooling_balance) → energy phase ends, momentum phase begins.

The problem the workstream solves: TRINITY's 1-D spherical interface under-counts the interface area — a real 3-D interface is fractal/corrugated (Lancaster 2021a,b), so 3-D sims measure Θ ≈ 0.9–0.99 while TRINITY's native θ sits at 0.2–0.6 for most GMCs and the trigger never fires.

2. The two knobs — which is which

f_A — the source-term (in-ODE) boost: cooling_boost_fA

inside the structure ODE:  du/dt → f_A · (du/dt)rad  only where T < 105.5 K
consistently, the loss integrals:  Lcool = L₁ + f_A·(L₂ + L₃)
effective loss the budget/trigger sees:  Lloss = L₁ + f_A·(L₂+L₃) + Lleak

Why it acts on (L₂+L₃) and not L₁ or Lleak: f_A is the 1-D projection of the fractal area multiplier, Aeff = f_A · 4πR₂². Extra interface area means proportionally more interface emission — and the layer that radiates it is the bubble-side conduction front (zones L₂+L₃) draped on the CD, i.e. the T < 105.5 K band (precision 2026-07-19, consistent with the §1 anatomy). The hot interior L₁ is volume emission (no area to multiply) and Lleak is bulk escape, not interface radiation — scaling either would be physically wrong, so both are deliberately excluded.

The crucial consequence — the structure responds. Because the boost lives inside the ODE, the solved T(r) changes: the interface layer gets cooler and denser, the conductive evaporation flux into the bubble falls (Ṁevap ↓ — the El-Badry Eq-47 sign, measured across the theta5s matrix), and the solved Lcool genuinely rises. θ is an output of physics, not an imposed number.

f_mix — the post-hoc multiplier: cooling_boost_mode='multiplier'

structure solved FIRST, unboosted → Lcool, then:  Leff = Lleak + f_mix · Lcool

The multiplier is applied after get_bubbleproperties returns — the T(r) structure, the evaporation Ṁ, everything inside is frozen at the unboosted solution; only the scalar number fed to the energy budget and the trigger is inflated. It answers "what if the 1-D solve under-counts the total by a constant factor" without letting the physics react. That's why the docs call it the frozen-structure limit of f_A.

3. Why f_mix measurably backfires (bench6, HPC)

Both knobs feed the same energy equation:

dEb/dt = Lmech − Lloss − P dV/dt

Under f_mix, the inflated Lloss drains Eb → bubble pressure Pb falls → the next step's unboosted structure solve returns a weaker Lcool (interface emission ∝ n² ∝ Pb²) → f_mix·Lcool shrinks even as f_mix is large. Extended fire thresholds complete the density gradient: 1 → 4 → 12 → 24 → 64 (n̄ = 2.28×10⁵ → 43).
