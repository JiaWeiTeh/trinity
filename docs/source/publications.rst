Acknowledging TRINITY
=====================

If you use TRINITY in any published work, please cite the TRINITY
method paper, Teh et al. (2026), `arXiv:2605.27517
<https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27517>`__ (BibTeX via
`ADS <https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2026arXiv260527517T/abstract>`__).

Legacy citations
----------------

TRINITY builds on the WARPFIELD framework. Users may also wish to cite:

* Rahner, D., Pellegrini, E. W., Klessen, R. S., & Glover, S. C. O.
  (2017), *MNRAS*, 470, 4453 — WARPFIELD 1.0.
  `ADS <https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2017MNRAS.470.4453R>`__
* Rahner, D., Pellegrini, E. W., Glover, S. C. O., & Klessen, R. S.
  (2019), *MNRAS*, 483, 2547 — WARPFIELD 2.0.
  `ADS <https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019MNRAS.483.2547R>`__

Stellar feedback rates are drawn by default from
`Starburst99 <https://www.stsci.edu/science/starburst99/>`_
(Leitherer et al. 1999); any stellar-population-synthesis table can be
supplied instead (see :ref:`sec-parameters`, *Stellar feedback (SPS)*).
